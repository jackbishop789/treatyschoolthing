Negotiation Location

Treaty 1 was negotiated at Lower Fort Garry, located near Winnipeg, Manitoba. This historic site was significant for discussions between the Crown and Indigenous leaders.

Communities Involved

The key communities involved in Treaty 1 include:

  • Peguis First Nation
  • Sagkeeng First Nation
  • Fisher River Cree Nation
  • Other Anishinaabe communities in the region

Historic Terms of the Treaty

Treaty 1 included promises such as:

  • Land allotment of 160 acres per family.
  • Annual payments to Indigenous peoples.
  • Rights to hunt and fish in traditional territories.

Source: Click For Source

What Makes Treaty 1 Different

Treaty 1 is unique as it was one of the first treaties signed in Canada. It emphasized a commitment to mutual respect and coexistence and set a precedent for future negotiations.
